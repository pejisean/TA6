Kinofilms




Kinofilms was founded by a group of passionate film enthusiasts who wanted to create a platform to showcase and celebrate local Filipino cinema.

The founders recognized that while the Philippines has a rich and vibrant film industry, many independent and arthouse films often struggled to find adequate distribution and reach audiences beyond the major commercial theaters. They saw an opportunity to provide these films a dedicated space where they could be appreciated and discovered by viewers.


The inspiration for Kinofilms came from the founders' own experiences as film lovers. They recalled the sense of discovery and connection they felt when watching thought-provoking local films in intimate, community-focused settings. They wanted to recreate that experience and share it with others.


One of the founders, a former film student, had been frustrated by the lack of exposure for Filipino directors and the dominance of Hollywood blockbusters in the country's cinemas. "I would go to the multiplex and see the same big-budget action movies playing on every screen. Where were the unique voices and stories from our own filmmakers?" she recalled.


Another founder, a cinephile who had traveled extensively, was inspired by the vibrant independent film scenes they had encountered in other countries. "In places like South Korea and Mexico, there were these wonderful small theaters that celebrated the local film culture. I wanted to bring that same spirit back home," he said.


So the founders pooled their resources and opened the first Kinofilms cinema, determined to shine a spotlight on Filipino cinema. They carefully curated a program of acclaimed local films, from festival favorites to hidden gems, and worked to build a loyal community of film lovers.


The response was overwhelmingly positive, with audiences grateful for the opportunity to discover films they may have otherwise missed. Kinofilms soon expanded to additional locations, becoming a vital hub for independent Filipino filmmaking .


At its core, Kinofilms was born out of a deep passion for cinema and a belief in the power of local storytelling. The founders wanted to create a space where Filipino films could thrive and where audiences could engage with the country's rich cinematic heritage . It was a labor of love, driven by a commitment to elevating and preserving the unique voice of Philippine cinema.
